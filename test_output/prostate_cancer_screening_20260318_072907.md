> This ERSPC paper teaches an important CONSORT lesson: comprehensive research data does not automatically equal transparent, structured reporting. The authors clearly understood their methods (randomization ratio varied by country, differential recruitment strategies, complex outcome adjudication), but failed to organize this in the hierarchical, upfront manner CONSORT requires. **Разбор пунктов с низкой оценкой:**

- **1a_title_identification:** The title states 'Screening and Prostate-Cancer Mortality in a Randomized European Study,' which clearly identifies it as randomized. However, CONSORT prefers the explicit word 'randomised' or 'randomized' to appear more prominently (ideally in opening phrase). Here it's embedded in descriptive text rather than leading the title.
- **1b_structured_abstract:** The abstract presents background, methods (study design, population, intervention, primary outcome), results (mortality rates, rate ratios, NNS), and conclusions. However, CONSORT requires more explicit structured headings (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions) in the abstract itself. This article uses paragraph format without clear subheadings, making it less structured than required.
- **2a_scientific_rationale:** The introduction explains that 'the effect of PSA-based screening on prostate-cancer mortality remains unclear' and notes the ERSPC was initiated 'to determine whether a reduction of 25% in prostate-cancer mortality could be achieved by PSA-based screening.' However, CONSORT wants fuller justification of why this question matters (epidemiological burden, current evidence gaps, clinical equipoise). The rationale is present but abbreviated.
- **4b_settings_locations:** The article states recruitment occurred in 'seven European countries' and lists them (Finland, Sweden, Netherlands, Belgium, Italy, Spain, Switzerland) with specific institutions mentioned in the Appendix. However, CONSORT requires this geographic/institutional detail in the Methods section itself, not relegated to appendix. The main text lacks specific hospital/center names.
- **6b_outcome_changes:** The article contains no explicit statement about whether primary or secondary outcomes were changed after trial initiation. No discussion of outcome modification, addition, or removal appears. This is a complete absence of required transparency about protocol deviations post-randomization.
- **7a_sample_size:** The article states 'The study had a power of 86% to show a statistically significant difference of 25% or more in prostate-cancer mortality with a P value of 0.05 among men who underwent screening, on the basis of follow-up through 2008' and briefly describes assumptions (82% compliance, 20% contamination). ## 🤖 ИИ-экспертиза

> For future large international trials: (1) Pre-specify and register all primary/secondary outcomes and subgroup analyses in a public registry upfront—this ERSPC trial would have benefited from explicit CONSORT 2010 checklist completion at submission; (2) Clarify control arm status (true no-intervention vs. usual care) in structured Methods, as natural screening background in control groups creates confounding that standard descriptive text fails to make transparent; (3) Consolidate randomization details (method, ratio justification, stratification by site and age) in a dedicated subsection rather than scattered across paragraphs, enabling reproducibility verification. The monitoring plan (mentioned) should also specify pre-planned stopping rules explicitly.

**Per-item LLM assessment:**

- **1a_title_identification:** The title states 'Screening and Prostate-Cancer Mortality in a Randomized European Study,' which clearly identifies it as randomized. However, CONSORT prefers the explicit word 'randomised' or 'randomized' to appear more prominently (ideally in opening phrase). Here it's embedded in descriptive text rather than leading the title.
- **1b_structured_abstract:** The abstract presents background, methods (study design, population, intervention, primary outcome), results (mortality rates, rate ratios, NNS), and conclusions. However, CONSORT requires more explicit structured headings (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions) in the abstract itself. This article uses paragraph format without clear subheadings, making it less structured than required.
- **2a_scientific_rationale:** The introduction explains that 'the effect of PSA-based screening on prostate-cancer mortality remains unclear' and notes the ERSPC was initiated 'to determine whether a reduction of 25% in prostate-cancer mortality could be achieved by PSA-based screening.' However, CONSORT wants fuller justification of why this question matters (epidemiological burden, current evidence gaps, clinical equipoise). The rationale is present but abbreviated.
- **4b_settings_locations:** The article states recruitment occurred in 'seven European countries' and lists them (Finland, Sweden, Netherlands, Belgium, Italy, Spain, Switzerland) with specific institutions mentioned in the Appendix. However, CONSORT requires this geographic/institutional detail in the Methods section itself, not relegated to appendix. The main text lacks specific hospital/center names.
- **6b_outcome_changes:** The article contains no explicit statement about whether primary or secondary outcomes were changed after trial initiation. No discussion of outcome modification, addition, or removal appears. This is a complete absence of required transparency about protocol deviations post-randomization.
- **7a_sample_size:** The article states 'The study had a power of 86% to show a statistically significant difference of 25% or more in prostate-cancer mortality with a P value of 0.05 among men who underwent screening, on the basis of follow-up through 2008' and briefly describes assumptions (82% compliance, 20% contamination). However, CONSORT requires explicit calculation formula, assumptions clearly laid out upfront, and explanation of why 25% effect size was chosen. Details are scattered and incomplete.
- **8a_randomization_method:** The text states 'men were assigned to either the screening group or the control group, without the use of blocks of numbers or stratification on the basis of random-number generators.' This tells what was NOT used but lacks specifics on what method WAS used (e.g., computer random number generator, permuted blocks, etc.). The mechanism is vague.
- **8b_randomization_type_restrictions:** The article mentions 'Within each country, men were assigned...without the use of blocks of numbers or stratification' and separately notes Finland used a different allocation ratio (1:1.5 instead of 1:1). However, CONSORT requires clear upfront description of randomization type (simple, blocked, stratified, adaptive) and any restrictions. The information is present but fragmented across sections.
- **11a_blinding:** The article states 'Causes of death were evaluated in a blinded fashion' but does not explicitly state who was blinded (outcome assessors only?) or whether participants/clinicians were blinded to allocation. PSA screening inherently cannot be blinded to participants, but CONSORT requires explicit statement of who was and was not blinded and why.
- **11b_intervention_similarity:** The article describes screening procedures in detail and mentions 'treatment of prostate cancer was performed according to local policies and guidelines' with a vague statement that 'equality of distribution of treatments...has been evaluated, with little indication of differences between the two study groups.' However, CONSORT requires explicit description of whether control group received any placebo/sham intervention or whether true no-intervention control was used. This distinction is entirely missing.
- **14a_recruitment_followup_dates:** The article provides dates by country in Table 1 (e.g., 'November 1993–March 2000' for Netherlands) and states 'Mortality follow-up...ended on December 31, 2006.' However, CONSORT requires this information clearly stated in Methods, not buried in tables. The overall trial initiation date ('early 1990s') is vague; specific start of enrollment across all sites is unclear.
- **14b_trial_stopping_reason:** The article states follow-up 'ended on December 31, 2006' as a 'uniform censoring date' chosen a priori. However, CONSORT requires explicit statement of whether this was a pre-planned endpoint (yes, appears to be) or early stopping due to efficacy/futility/harm. The monitoring committee 'had oversight...with a mandate to stop the trial on demonstrating a significant difference' but no discussion of whether they considered early stopping. The rationale for the specific December 2006 date is not explained.
- **18_subgroup_analyses:** The article includes Table 2 analyzing mortality 'according to age group' with a chi-square test for heterogeneity (P=0.49) and states in Discussion 'The trial was not powered to evaluate mortality differences between centers or for age subgroups.' There is also exploratory analysis of heterogeneity by study center (Table 3). However, CONSORT requires pre-specification of which subgroups were planned vs. exploratory. The article labels age analysis as showing 'no evidence of heterogeneity' but doesn't clearly distinguish pre-planned from post-hoc subgroup investigations.
- **22_interpretation_balance:** The Discussion acknowledges both benefits ('20% reduction in prostate-cancer mortality') and harms ('overdiagnosis...probably the most important adverse effects' with up to 50% overdiagnosis rate) and explicitly states 'the introduction of population-based screening must take into account population coverage, overdiagnosis, overtreatment, quality of life, cost, and cost-effectiveness.' However, CONSORT requires more balanced integration: while harms are mentioned, the quantification of harm-to-benefit ratio (48 additional treatments for 1 death prevented) could be emphasized equally with mortality benefit in the Discussion itself, not just Results.
